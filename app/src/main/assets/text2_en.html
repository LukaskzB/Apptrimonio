Legislation is a set of laws and norms that govern broad issues within a society. In the Brazilian Constitution, which is a set of legal norms, are the definition of public property and punishment for those who deteriorate these goods, emphasizing the importance of keeping them in good condition, because they must be used for the benefit of the collective, under penalty of responsibility.
   According to the information present in the Brazilian Constitution, in Art. 1st, of Law 4.717 of June 29, 1965, Public Property is the set of goods and rights of economic value, artistic, aesthetic, historical or tourist belonging to the entities of direct and indirect public administration.
   In the Brazilian Penal Code, about damage to public property, explicit in Art. 163, of Law No 2.848 of 7 December 1940, it is foreseen that destroying, disabling or deteriorating something of others results in penalty or detention of 1 to 6 months, or fine. If the crime is committed against the property of the Union, State, Municipality, public service concessionaire company or mixed economy society the penalty or detention is 6 months to 3 years and fine, plus the penalty corresponding to violence.
   However, each municipality can have its own laws that cover the depredation of public property, possibly with small modifications, and it is the responsibility of the public authorities to comply with such laws, in addition to the maintenance of damaged goods.
   The Federal Constitution, in its Article 225, explains that everyone has the right to the environment ecologically balanced, well of common use of the people and essential to the healthy quality of life, imposing to the Public Power and the collective the duty to defendand to preserve it for present and future generations.
