Taxes are wealth collected daily on collective expenses, being created by political entities of the State and assigned in the Federal Constitution. In general, the taxes collected come from property, income and consumption, the latter being a taxation of products necessary for survival, with fixed rates.
   The taxes collected on the income vary according to the value received by the worker. Taxes on property vary from the amount that is worth the good, being proportional quantities.
   After tax collection, the Federal Government redirects to the common good, maintaining various spheres of society, such as education, health, as well as social structures and programs, guaranteeing services to the population. These investments are defined in the Annual Budget Law, which determines the application of the funds that the Government has.
   An important area to which taxes are redirected is education, where the money collected from taxes is used to maintain and improve public schools, which have assets that are enjoyed by the entire school community.
   Tax collection is important to fund expenses and investments of the state, always aiming to ensure the well-being of the population, needing a well-structured planning for better use of money.
