Beyond the price contained in the purchase and sale of the object, there is also the sentimental value, often difficult to be measured. The symbolism we attribute to patrimony comes from the fact that our mind uses the object as an aid to the brain in order to rescue good things already felt.
   The sentimental value can be widely seen in the cultural heritage, where apparently simple pieces are auctioned by large financial values, due to the importance that the good represents for society.
   In another perspective, the sentimental importance can also be a useful mechanism to decrease the depredation of public assets in schools, where one of the objectives to be achieved together is to establish a link between students and the patrimony present in the institution.
